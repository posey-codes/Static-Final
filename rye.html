Rye


Similar to bourbon, rye must be created from a mash that is at least 51% rye. It also must be no more than 160 proof (or 80% alcohol) while also being distilled in charred oak barrels. It must be distilled for over 2 years to be considered "Straight Rye Whiskey".

Fun Fact: After prohibition, rye whiskey mostly dissapeared from consumption aside from a few minor brands. It wasn't until the 21st century that it started to make a comeback.

Bourbon is seemed as slightly "sweeter" than it's rye counterpart. It's said to add a spicy or fruity flavor instead.
